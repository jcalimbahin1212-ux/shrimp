The Industrial Revolution, which began in Britain in the late 18th century, was a period of great change that transformed largely rural, agrarian societies in Europe and America into industrialized, urban ones.

 

Before the Industrial Revolution, most people lived in small communities. Their daily lives revolved around farming and handmade goods. Manufacturing was often done in homes using hand tools or basic machines.

 

The introduction of new manufacturing processes between about 1760 and 1840 led to a dramatic increase in production output. Key developments included:

 

  • The transition from hand production methods to machines
  • New chemical manufacturing and iron production processes
  • The increasing use of steam power and water power
  • The development of machine tools

 

The textile industry was the first to adopt these innovations, followed quickly by iron and coal mining. The effects spread throughout Western Europe and North America during the 19th century, eventually affecting most of the world.|
